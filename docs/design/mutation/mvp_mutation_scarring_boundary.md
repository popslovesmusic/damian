# MVP Mutation Scarring Boundary

This document defines the foundational rules for environmental degradation caused by established Order (Mutation Scarring).

## Philosophy: Order Leaves a Mark

In the Tower, localized order is a foreign intrusion. The Tower's environment reacts to the presence of footholds not just with immediate pushback, but with long-term environmental degradation. This is **Mutation Scarring**. A foothold doesn't just exist; it "strains" the fabric of reality around it, creating hotspots where future mutations are more likely to be hazardous.

## Scarring Mechanics

Scarring Pressure is a deterministic measurement of localized environmental "stain":

1.  **Visibility Weight**: Higher visibility footholds (like Outposts) generate scar intensity faster than low-visibility ones.
2.  **Temporal Persistence**: Scarring increases the longer a foothold is held. Order is a cumulative stress on the Tower.
3.  **Localized Hotspots**: Scarring is tied to specific room nodes. Even if a foothold is lost or abandoned, the "scar" remains, making that node more dangerous for future traversal or reclamation.
4.  **Hazard Bias**: Local scarring materially increases the tactical hazard and mutation risk of its node, reinforcing the principle that rewards (dominion) carry lasting environmental costs.

## Identity Rules

To maintain engine integrity, mutation scarring follows these rules:

*   **Friction, not Erasure**: Scarring increases tactical friction (hazard levels) rather than deleting map segments.
*   **Maintenance Mitigation**: Effective upkeep and stabilization can slow the rate of scarring, but cannot erase the fundamental stress of Order.
*   **Recursive Consequence**: Scarring strengthens the Tower's recursive memory. A node that has been repeatedly claimed and lost becomes a "scarred wasteland," materially more difficult to hold in the future.

## Future Path

In Stage 016, this boundary will be implemented as a functional Scarring Stub. The Domain Dashboard will be updated to surface localized scar levels, ensuring that players can audit the environmental price of their territorial control.
