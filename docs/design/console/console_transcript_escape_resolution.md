# Console Transcript Escape Resolution Evidence

This document explains how the console transcript reporting system captures and records spatial escape resolution evidence.

## Overview

The `console_transcript_reporter` has been extended to capture evidence of the player's retreat attempts and the associated strategic outcomes. This ensures that every attempt to flee the Tower—including successes, partial escapes, and severe failures—is materially recorded and reviewable.

## Captured Evidence

The transcript now includes the following escape observability fields:

*   **`escape_resolutions_observed`**: An integer count of all processed escape attempts.
*   **`escape_successes_observed`**: Count of attempts that resulted in a safe retreat (`ESCAPE_SUCCESS`).
*   **`escape_partials_observed`**: Count of attempts that succeeded but with minor penalties (`ESCAPE_PARTIAL`).
*   **`escape_failures_observed`**: Count of attempts that resulted in hazardous consequences (e.g., resource loss or drops).
*   **`severe_escape_failures_observed`**: Specifically tracks attempts that resulted in a `DEFEAT_DROP`.
*   **`escape_residue_written_observed`**: A boolean flag confirming that flee attempts left a material mark on the Tower.
*   **`escape_mutation_pressure_observed`**: Indicates if failed retreats spiked the floor's environmental instability.
*   **`total_escape_mutation_pressure_delta`**: The cumulative mutation pressure generated by hazardous escapes.
*   **`escape_resource_losses_observed`**: An array of material supply losses (gold, potions) incurred during flight.
*   **`escape_pipeline_outcomes_observed`**: An array of the final outcome categories (`RETREAT_TO_HUB` or `DEFEAT_DROP`) processed by the engine.
*   **`escape_recoverability_preserved`**: A safety boolean confirming that no escape failure resulted in an unrecoverable dead state.
*   **`escape_resolution_summaries`**: An array of human-readable strings preserving the strategic context and outcome of every retreat attempt.

## Integration Flow

1.  **Escape Command**: The player executes the `escape` command.
2.  **Outcome Resolution**: The console resolves the attempt via `escape_resolution_stub`.
3.  **Payload Extraction**: The transcript reporter inspects the command result `payload` for the `escape_resolution` record.
4.  **Aggregation**:
    *   Resolution counts and outcome categories are updated.
    *   Material losses and pressure spikes are accumulated.
    *   Safety flags (recoverability, floor identity) are verified.
5.  **Audit Recording**: The aggregated evidence is written to the transcript JSON in `outputs/console_transcripts/`.

## Strategic Observability

By capturing escape consequences, auditors can review the "journey home" as a material strategically tradeoff. Transcripts reveal if players are successfully managing their retreat risk or if they are suffering compounding losses due to over-encumbrance or hazardous route choices. This reinforces the core principle that every decision in the Tower—including the decision to stop—carries a physical and auditable consequence.
