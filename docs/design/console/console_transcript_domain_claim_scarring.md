# Console Transcript Scarring and Targeting Evidence

This document explains how the console transcript reporting system captures the environmental and focused pushback against established Order.

## Overview

The `console_transcript_reporter` has been extended to record the physical and focused consequences of territory ownership. This ensures that localized environmental degradation (**Mutation Scarring**) and focused environmental attention (**Claim Targeting**) are materially captured and reviewable in session transcripts.

## Captured Evidence

The transcript now includes the following dashboard and claim observability fields:

### Mutation Scarring
*   **`scarred_nodes_observed`**: Count of unique nodes that have generated environmental stain.
*   **`highest_scar_intensity_observed`**: The peak environmental degradation (0.0 to 1.0) recorded among established footholds.
*   **`aggregate_hazard_bias_observed`**: The total tactical friction generated by localized scars.
*   **`scarring_summaries`**: Longitudinal records of how order is "staining" specific topographical nodes.

### Claim Targeting
*   **`highest_targeting_pressure_observed`**: The peak focused attention (0.0 to 1.0) recorded for any single foothold.
*   **`foothold_destabilization_events_observed`**: Count of instances where targeting pressure caused a foothold to enter a critical risk state.
*   **`targeting_summaries`**: Human-readable records of how specific footholds are being pressured by the Tower.

## Integration Flow

1.  **Status/Claim Loop**: The player executes `status` or `claim` commands.
2.  **Evidence Extraction**: The transcript reporter inspects the command payloads for `mutation_scarring` and `targeting_pressure` records.
3.  **Aggregation**: Data is peaked, counted, and recorded to provide a timeline of the "consequence health" of established order.
4.  **Audit Trail**: The results are preserved in the JSON transcript for post-run analysis.

## Strategic Observability

By capturing this evidence, transcripts reveal the hidden costs of territorial dominance. Auditors can analyze whether the strategic relief of a foothold is worth the long-term environmental degradation (Scarring) and the escalating focused hostility (Targeting) it invites. This reinforces the core principle that the Tower is not a passive map, but an active participant that reacts to the player's attempts to impose Order.
