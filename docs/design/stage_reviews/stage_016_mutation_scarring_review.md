# Stage 016: Mutation Scarring and Claim Targeting Review

## Overview
Stage 016 materialized the physical and focused consequences of established Order. This involved implementing **Mutation Scarring** (localized environmental degradation) and **Claim Targeting** (focused environmental pushback), ensuring that territorial footholds are active sources of escalating risk rather than passive strategic safe-zones.

## Identity Status
**STRONG ALIGNMENT**
The engine has successfully moved beyond general environmental pushback to a state where the Tower "notices" and "reacts to" specific player choices. By measurement of environmental stain (Scarring) and focused attention (Targeting), the system reinforces the core principle: **Order is an artificial stress that invites focused entropy.** The Tower is now an active participant that pressures, distorts, and degrades Order.

## Major Strengths
1.  **Localized Environmental Memory:** Mutation Scarring creates a lasting topographical cost for established Order, even after a foothold is lost.
2.  **Focused Strategic Pressure:** Claim Targeting turns visibility into a direct liability, materially increasing maintenance costs and destabilizing over-exposed territory.
3.  **Material Hazard Feedback:** The introduction of "Hazard Bias" from scarring ensures that owning territory has a physical impact on tactical difficulty.
4.  **Meta-Strategic Integration:** Perfect continuity with the Domain Dashboard and transcripts, ensuring that environmental stain and focused risk are fully auditable.
5.  **Strict Scope Containment:** Achieved environmental pushback without introducing enemy AI runtimes, rendered sieges, or physical structural damage.

## Major Risks
1.  **Snowballing Environmental Hazard:** If scarring is too aggressive, nodes could become "permanently unusable," potentially creating soft-locks for players attempting to rebuild.
2.  **Information Overload:** The survival dashboard is becoming increasingly dense with metrics (Reclamation, Scarring, Targeting). We must ensure at-a-glance clarity is maintained.
3.  **Complexity of Mitigation:** Currently, the player can only "pay" to maintain territory. There are no active meta-strategic tools to "clean" a scar or "hide" from targeting.

## Balance Questions
*   Is the upkeep penalty from targeting high enough to force meaningful abandonment choices?
*   Should scarring intensity decay slowly over time if no order is present?
*   How does scarring interact with the generation of survivor marks? Does order destroy easter eggs?

## Required Follow-ups
1.  **Implement Manual Route Selection (Stage 017):** Now that territory is risky, give the player direct agency to select their routes through the room graph to avoid or stabilize hotspots.
2.  **Refine Destabilization Outcomes:** Develop specific mutation events that trigger when a foothold becomes `OVERRUN`.
3.  **Dashboard UI Polish:** Review the aggregation of survival pressure to ensure high-priority hazards (like `CRITICAL` targeting) are surfaced effectively.

## Next Stage Recommendation
Proceed to **Stage 017: Manual Route Selection and Spatial Agency**. Now that we have established the risks and stains of territory, we must give the player the direct agency to navigate through the room graph, enabling them to strategically bypass high-risk scars or proactively reach decaying footholds.
