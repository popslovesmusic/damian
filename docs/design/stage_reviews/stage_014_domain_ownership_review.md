# Stage 014: Domain Ownership and Local Footholds Review

## Overview
Stage 014 shifted the engine's focus toward meta-strategic territory management. This involved defining the Domain Ownership Boundary and implementing a minimal Domain Claim Stub that calculates material hazards for establishing localized strategic footholds (e.g., Recovery Anchors).

## Identity Status
**STRONG ALIGNMENT**
Domain claims have successfully established a framework for **meta-strategic order** without creating "safe bases." Each claim carries material operational burdens (Maintenance Pressure) and environmental risks (Visibility Pressure) that scale with floor depth. The engine enforces the principle: **owning territory is a strategic investment in future risk mitigation, not an erasure of current consequence.**

## Major Strengths
1.  **Differentiated Foothold Biases:** Successfully implemented multiple claim types (Supply Caches, Repair Stations, etc.) each with unique strategic tradeoffs, avoiding generic territory systems.
2.  **Scalable Operational Burden:** Maintenance and visibility pressures correctly scale with floor depth and established complexity, ensuring deep territory remains a material challenge.
3.  **Preservation of Hostility:** Localized ownership does not bypass tactical risk or residue generation; it only manages the *impact* of that risk, reinforcing the Tower's inherent strategic hostility.
4.  **Meta-Strategic Integration:** Perfect alignment with the Domain Dashboard and transcripts, ensuring that territory establishment is fully observable and audit-ready.
5.  **Strict Scope Containment:** Achieved territory management without introducing base-building runtimes, voxel editing, or rendered housing.

## Major Risks
1.  **Complexity Creep:** Managing a growing network of localized footholds might become cognitively exhausting or feel like a chore rather than a strategic choice.
2.  **Information Density:** As more claim-specific metrics are added, the dashboard may become cluttered, potentially obscuring the most critical survival hazards.
3.  **Upkeep Lag:** Currently, upkeep is defined as a pressure metric but not yet materialized as an active shard-drain runtime, which may provide a temporary false sense of safety.

## Balance Questions
*   Is the "Visibility Pressure" high enough to make deep-floor claims feel truly dangerous to establish?
*   Should the benefits of a "Recovery Anchor" be more granular (e.g., specific to durability restoration vs. potion efficiency)?
*   How should ownership interact with "Defeat Drops"? Does a foothold provide a safer recovery path or just another asset to lose?

## Required Follow-ups
1.  **Define MVP Domain Upkeep Boundary:** Implement the active runtime that materially deducts "Stability Shards" per run to maintain dominion.
2.  **Implement 'Scarring' Logic:** Develop mutation behaviors that specifically target and degrade established footholds based on visibility risk.
3.  **Integrate Topological Advantage:** Reward players for navigating through their own network of footholds during spatial traversal.

## Next Stage Recommendation
Proceed to **Stage 015: Define MVP Domain Upkeep Boundary**. Now that we can establish localized order, we must implement the material costs of maintaining that order to ensure that territory ownership remains a strategic burden.
