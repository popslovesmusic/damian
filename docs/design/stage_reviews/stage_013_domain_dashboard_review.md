# Stage 013: Domain Dashboard and Survival Cognition Review

## Overview
Stage 013 focused on meta-strategic oversight. This involved defining the Domain Dashboard Boundary and implementing the `domain_dashboard_snapshot_builder` to aggregate material evidence from all core engine subsystems—tactical combat, spatial traversal, inventory burden, and floor history—into a unified, readable state.

## Identity Status
**STRONG ALIGNMENT**
The dashboard functions as strategic cognition infrastructure. It successfully transforms disparate material hazards and recursive history into a unified survival profile, enabling proactive meta-strategic management without removing the inherent risk of the Tower. It reinforces the core principle: **survival is an auditable strategic choice**.

## Major Strengths
1.  **Unified Meta-Strategic Visibility:** Successfully aggregated tactical hazards (combat/traversal) with material burdens (inventory/repair) and environmental history into a single, cohesive report.
2.  **Cognitive Clarity:** The readable summaries and structured snapshots make complex weighted pressures understandable for players, designers, and auditors.
3.  **Deterministic Grounding:** All dashboard evidence is derived strictly from known session state, preserving operational uncertainty and ensuring engine integrity.
4.  **Audit Trail Maturity:** The inclusion of snapshots in transcripts allows for longitudinal analysis of run health and compounding pressures across multiple sessions.
5.  **Scope Containment:** Achieved meta-strategic oversight without the need for graphic UI frameworks, animations, or real-time overlays.

## Major Risks
1.  **Heuristic Over-simplification:** The "Recoverability" and "Critical Pressure" heuristics are currently basic. They might oversimplify complex strategic situations or provide a false sense of safety/danger.
2.  **Information Density:** As the engine continues to expand (e.g., into Domain Ownership or Protection), the dashboard may become cluttered, reducing its "at-a-glance" effectiveness.
3.  **Passive Oversight:** Currently, the dashboard only *surfaces* information; it doesn't yet provide active meta-strategic *actions* to mitigate the pressures it reports.

## Balance Questions
*   Are the current weightings in the pressure summary (Combat 35%, Mutation 25%, etc.) accurately reflecting actual survival difficulty as perceived by playtesters?
*   Should the dashboard reveal "invisible" residue pressure (strategy repetition) or only focus on materialized mutation levels?
*   How can we prevent the dashboard from becoming a "safety net" that reduces the visceral fear of spatial exploration?

## Required Follow-ups
1.  **Define MVP Domain Ownership Boundary:** Transition from observing compounding consequences to actively owning and managing segments of the Tower Domain.
2.  **Refine Survival Heuristics:** Develop more sophisticated ways to assess session recoverability based on longitudinal transcript evidence.
3.  **Dynamic Weighting Audit:** Conduct a playtest audit to ensure the pressure summary accurately weights the factors most likely to lead to run failure.

## Next Stage Recommendation
Proceed to **Stage 014: Define MVP Domain Ownership Boundary**. Now that we can cognitively read the Tower's meta-strategic state, we must define the framework for the player to transition from a mere survivor to a strategic owner of the Tower Domain.
