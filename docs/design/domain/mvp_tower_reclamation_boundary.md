# MVP Tower Reclamation Pressure Boundary

This document defines the rules for the Tower's environmental pushback against established Order (Domain Footholds).

## Philosophy: Order Generates Resistance

The Tower is a naturally chaotic and entropic environment. Establishing localized "Order" (footholds) creates an imbalance that the Tower's environment actively attempts to correct. This counter-force is materially represented as **Reclamation Pressure**.

## Reclamation Mechanics

Reclamation Pressure is not a random attack, but a deterministic aggregation of environmental "irritation":

1.  **Visibility Contribution**: High-visibility footholds (like Outposts) attract more environmental attention.
2.  **Decay Contribution**: Neglected footholds (those in a `DECAYING` state) weaken the fabric of Order, making the area more susceptible to reclamation.
3.  **Depth Contribution**: The deeper the floor, the stronger the Tower's natural reclamation drive.
4.  **Aggregate Hazard**: These factors are weighted and summed into a bounded value (0.0 to 1.0), classified into **Reclamation Bands** (STABLE, IRRITATED, HOSTILE, VOLATILE, CRITICAL).

## Identity Rules

To maintain the engine's core strategic identity, reclamation follows these rules:

*   **Mutation Bias**: Higher Reclamation Pressure materially biases the mutation generator, increasing the likelihood of mutations that target established order or escalate tactical risk.
*   **Recursive Consequence**: Successful reclamation events "scar" the environment, leaving behind residue that increases future Reclamation Pressure even if Order is restored.
*   **Strategic Uncertainty**: While deterministic, Reclamation Pressure preserves uncertainty by influencing the *type* and *timing* of environmental responses rather than providing a simple "wave timer."

## Future Path

In Stage 015, this boundary will be implemented as a functional Reclamation Stub. The Domain Dashboard will be updated to surface this pressure as a distinct survival hazard, forcing players to balance their conquest against the escalating resistance of the Tower.
