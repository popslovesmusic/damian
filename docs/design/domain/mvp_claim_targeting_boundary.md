# MVP Claim Targeting Boundary

This document defines the rules for focused environmental pressure against established Order (Claim Targeting).

## Philosophy: Visibility is a Target

In the Tower, building order is not a quiet act. Establishing a foothold generates a "signal" that the environment actively notices. **Claim Targeting** is the measurement of this focused attention. Unlike general Reclamation Pressure (which is floor-wide), Targeting Pressure is specific to a single foothold, measuring how likely it is to be the next subject of environmental "correction."

## Targeting Mechanics

Targeting Pressure is a deterministic aggregation of local "irritation" factors:

1.  **Visibility Pressure**: The primary driver. High-visibility claims (like Outposts) attract more focused attention than low-visibility ones.
2.  **Local Scarring**: Nodes that are heavily scarred are "unstable ground," making the claims on them easier for the Tower to target.
3.  **Reclamation Drive**: The floor-wide reclamation pressure acts as a multiplier for individual targeting.
4.  **Destabilization**: When targeting pressure crosses critical thresholds, it triggers **Destabilization Events**, which materially increase upkeep costs and reduce strategic benefits.

## Identity Rules

To maintain engine integrity, claim targeting follows these rules:

*   **Pressure, not Deletion**: Targeting must pressure, distort, and degrade footholds rather than instantly erasing them.
*   **Upkeep Escalation**: Targeted claims are more difficult to maintain, materially represented by an increase in their per-run Stability Shard cost.
*   **Auditable Risk**: Targeting risk levels remain clearly visible in the Domain Dashboard, reinforcing the principle that rewards carry measurable strategic burden.

## Future Path

In Stage 016, this boundary will be implemented as a functional Targeting Stub. The `maintain` command will be updated to handle the escalating costs of targeted claims, turning territory management into a dynamic struggle against focused entropy.
