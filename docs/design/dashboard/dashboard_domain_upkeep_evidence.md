# Dashboard Domain Upkeep Evidence

This document explains how territory maintenance and decay are integrated into the meta-strategic Domain Dashboard snapshots.

## Overview

The `domain_dashboard_snapshot_builder` has been extended to surface the "Maintenance Health" of established order. This ensures that the material cost and state of territory management—including decaying and overrun footholds—are auditably tracked in the meta-strategic oversight layer.

## Integrated Evidence

The dashboard snapshot now provides visibility into the following upkeep-related metrics:

1.  **State Ratios**: Separate counts for **ACTIVE**, **DECAYING**, and **OVERRUN** claims. This allows players and auditors to see at a glance how much territory is being neglected.
2.  **Peak Maintenance Burden**: Surfaces the highest maintenance cost currently active among established footholds.
3.  **Compounding Risk**: By tracking **DECAYING** counts, the dashboard signals an impending escalation in **Tower Reclamation Pressure** and visibility risk.

## Strategic Cognition

Surfacing decay counts in the dashboard forces the player to acknowledge the consequences of their resource management. A player who has many "DECAYING" claims will see their maintenance risk increase, reinforcing the principle that order is an Natural state that requires material investment.

## Identity Preservation

*   **Audit, not Action**: The dashboard surfaces the decay state but does not provide the mechanism to fix it. Fixes must be executed through the material strategic loop (e.g., the `maintain` command).
*   **Hostility Preservation**: The dashboard tracks whether neglected footholds are correctly attracting more visibility and higher mutation risk.
*   **Recursive Memory**: The presence of **OVERRUN** claims in the dashboard serves as a persistent reminder of failed maintenance, contributing to the "net economy" of the run.
